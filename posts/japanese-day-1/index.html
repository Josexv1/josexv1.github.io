Usage of です、は、の、か、時(とき)…

I cheated and already know about these particles, so I’ll try to sum everything here.

です is used as the verb to (be) For example

  • 私(わたし)はホセです。 I am Jose
  • 時計(とけい)です。 it’s a clock

は marks the topic we’re talking about.

To properly explain this particle we will need a whole book, because it’s kind of hard to understand and compare to the particle が, but Tokini Andy did an AWESOME job to make it short and easy to understand in this video

の basically means that something belongs to another

  • XのY means that Y belongs to X 僕(ぼく)の時計(とけい) my clock
  • 武(たけ)のお母(かあ)さん Takeshi’s mom.

か basically converts the phrase to a question, added to the end of the question, can also be added in the middle to choose options

  • 青(あお)いか赤(あか)いか blue or red?

時(とき) is the time particle　

  • 7時(じ)です
  • １１時半(じはん)です
  • １８時(じ)です。

I told you that I cheated and I tested the Anki deck beforehand, and it was really hard to remember some of the Kanji in the decks.

Some of the harder Kanji were.

  • 留学生(りゅうがくせい)　International Student
  • 専攻(せんこう)　　Major
  • 歳(とし)　　Age, like 12 years old
  • 年生(ねんせい)　　year, like year in school, 3rd year, 2nd year
  • 電話(でんわ)番号(ばんごう)　Phone number, I remembered the first kanji 電(でん) because it means electricity, and many electronic things has this kanji, like 携帯(けいたい)電話(でんわ) mobile phone, in this case 電話(でんわ) was a mix of electricity (電(でん)) and talk, speech (話(ばなし)), meaning cell phone, or just phone, I was dumb enough not to recognize the kanji 番号(ばんごう) that was at the TOP of the book, meaning number. But oh well, now I know.
